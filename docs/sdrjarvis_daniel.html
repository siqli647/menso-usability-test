SDR Jarvis usability test

Founder-led outbound breaks when the review loop hides the proof.

Menso tested SDR Jarvis through Daniel Kowalski, a majority-segment solo technical founder. The product solves a real distribution pain, but Daniel could not verify whether SDR Jarvis found the right people or wrote sendable emails because history, lead status, and draft review were not inspectable enough.

Top Insights

01

Real demand is strong: founder-led outbound needs a shortcut

Daniel immediately understands the value proposition: define ICP, find leads, generate personalized drafts, then approve before sending. This maps directly to a bootstrapped technical founder who needs first outbound motion without becoming an SDR-tools expert.

02

Trust breaks at lead relevance

The campaign targeted Engineering Manager / VP Engineering, but the visible leads included DevOps Engineer, Software Developer, Python Developer, and Cloud Developer. For this persona, lead fit is not a nice-to-have; it is the product.

03

Approval history is not reviewable

The Approvals page showed one approved and several rejected items, but history rows were not clickable. Daniel could not inspect the full draft, lead context, or rejection reason, so he could not learn whether the AI produced sendable messages.

04

Dry-run and Archived states need an audit trail

The active campaign had 5 researched leads, 0 sent, 0 pending, and all leads archived. Without clear reasons for archived status, the user cannot tell whether SDR Jarvis made a bad sourcing call, the campaign was only a dry run, or prior manual actions changed the state.

05

Conversion risk: the value proof is hidden

A bootstrapped founder with limited runway needs to see one credible lead and one credible draft quickly. If the first review loop becomes Campaigns -> Leads -> Approvals navigation without accessible draft details, the product feels untrustworthy even if the underlying automation worked.

Evidence From The Run

MAJOR · expectation_mismatch

As a fast-moving founder, I need to quickly review past decisions to gauge AI quality. The Approval history items are not clickable, so I can't see the lead details or email drafts that were approved or rejected. I expected to click into them for full context, but instead, I have to navigate to other sections to piece together information. This breaks my flow and wastes time.

Make each approval history item clickable, opening a panel that shows the full lead info and generated email draft. This aligns with my expectation of drilling down into specific decisions without leaving the page.

CRITICAL · trust_anxiety

I'm here to evaluate lead relevance, and I see 'DevOps Engineer' and 'Software Developer' as leads when my campaign explicitly targets 'Engineering Manager' and 'VP Engineering'. This mismatch makes me question the AI's ability to understand my ICP. If it can't find the right titles, the entire tool is useless for my cold outbound needs.

Improve lead source filtering to strictly match ICP criteria, and display a clear reason for each lead's inclusion (e.g., keyword match, job description analysis). Provide an option to feed back why a lead is irrelevant so the model learns.

Model-Generated Signals

  • workflow_fit: Non-clickable approval history items break the founder's review workflow, forcing him to navigate to other sections to understand past decisions, which wastes time and causes frustration.
  • trust: The lead sourcing doesn't match the specified ICP (Engineering Manager), including leads like 'DevOps Engineer', undermining trust in the AI's understanding of target personas.
  • confusion: The 'Archived' status on leads is ambiguous without an audit trail, leaving the founder unsure why leads were removed and whether the AI was at fault or a manual action.
  • value_signal: Without access to generated email drafts in the approval history, the founder cannot assess personalization quality, which is critical for deciding if the tool meets his cold outbound needs.
  • conversion_risk: The combination of poor lead relevance, inaccessible review tools, and ambiguity in lead status makes the product feel untrustworthy and inefficient, risking churn for a bootstrapped founder with limited runway.

Product Recommendations

  1. Make every Approval history item clickable, opening a side panel with lead profile, source context, generated email draft, decision history, and status reason.
  2. Tighten ICP matching around job title seniority and show why each lead matched the ICP. Let users mark why a lead is irrelevant so sourcing can improve.
  3. Differentiate Archived, Rejected, Approved, Pending, Dry run, and Sent with clear explanations and timestamps.
  4. Add a post-run review screen that summarizes: leads found, leads rejected, drafts generated, drafts approved, and next action.
  5. For first-time founders, show one sample lead plus one sample personalized email before asking them to trust a full pipeline run.
